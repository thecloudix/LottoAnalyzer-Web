LottoAnalyzer — Manual

A statistical exploration tool for historical Powerball draws. This is an educational app for studying patterns in past results — not a betting product, and it does not predict future winning numbers.

1. What this app does

LottoAnalyzer studies the history of Powerball draws and surfaces patterns: which white-ball numbers come up most often, which ones are overdue, which pairs co-occur, and how a given combination of numbers compares to historical norms.

The app ships with 606 historical draws built in (June 2022 through April 2026) so it works fully offline. When you tap Refresh, it pulls newer draws from the public NY State Open Data API.

This is a study tool, not a prediction engine. Lottery draws are independent random events — past results have no bearing on future ones. The app helps you explore the historical record, nothing more.

2. Installing and opening the app

  1. Install LottoAnalyzer from the Google Play Store on any device running Android 8.0 (Oreo) or newer.
  2. Open the app from your launcher. The first screen you'll see is the Frequency view, showing how often each white ball has been drawn.
  3. No account, no sign-in, no setup. The 606-draw dataset is already inside the app.

3. Navigating — the hamburger drawer

Tap the hamburger icon (☰) in the top-left corner to open the navigation drawer. From there you can jump to any of the eight screens. The drawer always shows your current screen highlighted in accent orange.

The drawer is the only way to switch screens — there is no bottom tab bar. This keeps every screen one tap away even on small phones.

4. The eight screens

Frequency

A bar list of all 69 white balls, ranked by how often they've been drawn within the active date filter. The most-drawn number sits at the top with the longest bar; the least-drawn at the bottom. Useful for spotting hot numbers in the historical record.

Due

The flip side of Frequency. Numbers are ranked by how many draws have passed since each one last appeared. The number with the longest gap is at the top — the most overdue. Each row also shows the date of the last appearance.

Reminder: overdue numbers are not "due" in any predictive sense — every draw is independent. This is purely descriptive.

Top Picks

A leaderboard view that combines hot-frequency and overdue-rank into a single percentile score per number. The five highest-percentile white balls and the top Powerball are surfaced here. Think of it as a "what does the historical record point to" digest.

Pairs

The most frequent two-ball co-occurrences across the dataset. Each row shows a pair of white balls and how many draws contain both. Useful if you're curious which numbers tend to show up together.

Score (Score-a-Combo)

Type in 5 white balls (1–69) and 1 Powerball (1–26). The app computes the same score the engine would give that combo against the dataset, broken down by component. Tap the ? next to any component name to see the formula and what the number means.

Cross-check combo: with the default settings, the combo 24 28 33 47 64 14 should always score 99.64 overall. If you ever get a different number, you've changed the date filter or the settings — reset them and try again.

Suggest

Asks the app to generate one combination for you, optimised for whichever scoring components you have enabled. Pick a generation mode — Hot, Due, Balanced, Random, or Tournament — and tap Generate. The result appears with a full score breakdown.

Best Combos (Tournament results)

Shows the top 5 highest-scoring unique combinations from the most recent tournament run. If you haven't run one, this view is empty. Run one from the Suggest screen.

Settings

The control panel for the scoring engine. Toggle each of the 9 components on or off, and slide each weight from 0 to 200. See Settings below for what each component does.

5. How the score is calculated

Every combination gets an overall score from 0 to 100, with two-decimal precision (e.g. 99.64). The overall score is a weighted average of up to 9 component scores. Each component is itself scored 0–100.

By default, the first 5 components are enabled at weight 100; the last 4 are disabled. Here's what each one measures:

ComponentWhat it measuresDefault
White avg freqHow often your 5 white balls have appeared, averaged. Higher = hotter numbers.On (100)
Powerball freqHow often your Powerball has appeared.On (100)
SumWhether the sum of your 5 whites falls in the historical sweet spot (~100–200).On (100)
Odd / EvenHow balanced your odd/even split is. 2-and-3 or 3-and-2 scores best.On (100)
Low / HighHow balanced your low (1–34) vs high (35–69) split is.On (100)
Chi-squaredHow well your numbers' historical frequencies match a uniform distribution.Off
Z-scoreStandardised distance of each number's frequency from the mean.Off
Overdue probabilityGeometric/Poisson estimate of how unlikely the current dry spell is.Off
Sum GaussianHow close the sum is to the historical mean, weighted by the historical std dev.Off

For the formal formulas and an example calculation per component, tap the ? next to the component name on the Score or Settings screen.

6. Settings — toggles and weights

For every scoring component you have two controls:

  • Toggle — turn the component on or off entirely. Off components contribute nothing to the overall score.
  • Weight slider (0–200) — relative importance of this component versus the others. The default is 100. Drag to 200 to double a component's relative pull, drag to 0 to mute it without disabling the toggle.

Settings persist on your device (via Android DataStore). They survive app restarts. If you want to start fresh, uninstall and reinstall.

Warning: changing the weights changes every score in the app — Suggest, Score, Top Picks, and Best Combos all use these settings. The cross-check combo only scores 99.64 with the defaults.

7. The date filter

At the top of most screens is a filter bar with date-range pills:

  • All — every draw in the dataset.
  • 5Y / 2Y / 1Y / 6M / 3M — the most recent 5 years, 2 years, 1 year, 6 months, or 3 months.
  • Custom — opens a date-range picker; pick a start and end date.

The filter is applied across all screens: Frequency, Due, Top Picks, Pairs, and the scoring engine all recompute against only the draws inside the range. This is the right tool for asking "what's been hot this year?" or "what was the picture in 2023?".

8. Refreshing the dataset

Tap the Refresh button at the top of any screen. The app:

  1. Connects to data.ny.gov (the New York State Open Data API).
  2. Pulls every Powerball draw on file — typically a few hundred KB.
  3. Merges the result into a local cache on your device, then recomputes statistics.

This is the only network call the app makes, and the only time it's made. If you never tap Refresh, the app stays fully offline and uses the 606 draws shipped with the build.

If the network is unavailable, Refresh fails silently and the app continues using the existing cache (or the embedded data if there's no cache yet).

9. Tournament mode

Open the Suggest screen and pick the Tournament mode. Set an iteration count (up to 1,000,000) and tap Run. The app:

  1. Generates that many candidate combinations using the Suggest algorithm.
  2. Scores each one using your current settings and date filter.
  3. Keeps the top 5 unique combinations and shows them in Best Combos.

The runner is chunked so the UI stays responsive — a progress bar tracks the run. You can leave the screen and come back; results land in Best Combos when finished.

A million-iteration run takes a few seconds on a recent device, longer on older hardware. Lower the count if it lags.

10. Data, privacy, and permissions

The app collects nothing about you. Specifically:

  • No analytics, no crash reporting, no advertising SDKs, no social SDKs.
  • No account, no sign-in.
  • The only network request is to data.ny.gov when you tap Refresh.
  • Permissions requested: INTERNET and ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE only.

What's stored on your device:

  • The cached draw list (refreshed when you tap Refresh).
  • Your scoring settings (toggles and weights).

Both are removed when you uninstall the app. The full privacy policy is available alongside this manual.

12. Troubleshooting

The Refresh button does nothing

Check your internet connection. The app contacts data.ny.gov; if the device has no network or the API is temporarily down, Refresh fails silently. Existing data and cached draws remain available offline.

The cross-check combo doesn't score 99.64

Open Settings and reset to defaults: first 5 components on at weight 100, last 4 off. Also confirm the date filter is set to "All". The 99.64 reference assumes both.

Tournament progress bar stalls

Larger iteration counts (500k+) take longer on older devices. Wait it out, or lower the count. The chunked runner won't freeze the UI but it does take wall-clock time.

I want to see draws older than June 2022

The embedded dataset starts there. Older draws aren't included to keep the app size small, and the NY State API only goes back to that period as well. There are no plans to extend it.
